Origins


During the medieval period, taverns, inns, and public houses served as the primary locations for food outside of the home. These establishments offered food and lodging to travelers and were often the focal point of community life. Meals were usually served in communal settings, and the quality and variety of food could vary widely.

story

The restaurant’s journey was not without challenges. During World War II, Le Jardin Secret faced difficult times. The scarcity of ingredients and the impact of the war on Parisian life meant that the restaurant had to adapt. Marie’s resilience shone through as she adjusted the menu to include simpler dishes and embraced the spirit of community, offering meals to those in need.
